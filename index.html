Why does pricing matter?

The profit formula
Profit = Sales Price × Units sold − Variable Cost × Units sold − Fixed Cost
Profit = SP × X − VC × X − FC
SP = Sales Price — what you charge per unit X = Units sold — how many you sell VC = Variable Cost — cost per unit (materials, delivery…) FC = Fixed Cost — costs that don’t change (rent, salaries…)
Baseline — your starting numbers
Sales Price (SP)
100
Units sold (X)
1,000
Variable Cost (VC)
10
Fixed Cost (FC)
10,000
Baseline Profit = (100 × 1,000) − (10 × 1,000) − 10,000 = 80,000
Now you — click a lever, then slide it
Raise the price
Only SP changes — everything else stays the same

Cut variable costs

Price is the most powerful lever.
At +10%, a price increase adds +10,000 to profit — that is 10× more than cutting variable costs (+1,000) and more than selling 10% extra units (+9,000). Price goes straight to the bottom line because it doesn’t create any extra cost.
Impact of a 1% improvement on profit (1,200 companies)
Profit driver Profit impact of 1% improvement Improvement needed to double profit
Price11.0%9.1%
Variable costs7.2%13.7%
Unit sales3.7%27.1%
Fixed costs2.7%37.1%

To double profit through price alone, you only need a 9.1% price increase. To achieve the same by selling more units, you would need to grow sales by 27.1%.
